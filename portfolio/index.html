About Randy Noxon

I build practical AI workflows for messy real-world problems: conversation repair, creative production, artifact recovery, local-first tools, and remote browser access.

My work is hands-on. I build lightweight browser-based AI tools, test how consumer and developer AI systems fail, design prompt repair workflows, and document repeatable processes that real people can actually use.

Recent projects include Twis Holo Workshop, a local-first AI creative workspace deployed through Cloudflare Pages with Workers AI away-mode access; Harness Card, a conversation repair tool for fixing AI drift, lost constraints, loops, and unsupported assumptions; and Thousand-Year Hangover, a source-governed creative AI workflow for long-form writing, visual prompts, music concepts, and narration planning.

I do not present myself as a machine learning engineer or model-training specialist. My strength is practical AI judgment: testing outputs, preserving constraints, improving prompts, documenting workflows, and turning messy real-world needs into usable AI systems.
